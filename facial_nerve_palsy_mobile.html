⚠️ Central Palsy — Pitfalls
• Pontine lesions (AICA, MS, glioma) produce LMN pattern despite CNS location → check Peripheral tab, Zone ①
• Frontal lesion: voluntary palsy only; emotional/spontaneous smile preserved (dissociation)
• Contralateral UMN signs favour central strongly
• Sudden isolated palsy without prodrome — image before labelling Bell's
• Bilateral facial palsy is almost never central

🔑 Peripheral — Clinical Pearls
Sub-localise by associated features:
Dry eye → lesion above geniculate ganglion
Hyperacusis → lesion above nerve to stapedius
Taste loss (ant ⅔ tongue) → lesion above chorda tympani
All spared → distal to stylomastoid foramen

Imaging indications (don't wait):
Gradual / slow onset · No recovery at 3 months · Recurrent palsy · Associated cranial neuropathies · Trauma · Palpable parotid mass

Bilateral = systemic until proven otherwise:
GBS · Lyme · Sarcoidosis · HIV · Leptomeningeal mets
